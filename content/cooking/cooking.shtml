Introduction

Just a little page to encourage people that cooking food is more than just standing near a pot and stirring it. I will offer a few suggestions to improve your cooking.

Quick tips

Use butter for fryups and olive oil for italian cooking

Ok, butter admittedly is not very good for you, but it makes it taste 10x better!

On the subject of butter, you don't neccessarily have to keep it in the fridge I find. A nice dish will keep butter fresh and soft enough for a few days use at the very least.

You can mix butter and oil. Butter has the taste, but it cannot hold the heat like oil does. Use clarified butter, as unclarified leads to a burnt taste.

Cooking stuff when the oil is not hot leads the ingrediants to lose their taste. Frying is the best way to learn howto cook, as it generally tastes good and for geeks it allows high visibility of the cooking process.

Buy fresh stuff

I used to hate it when my father would take me to shops directly after a hard day at school. I begged him to buy frozen food like the rest of the kids' families were doing. It did not take me long to figure out how important fresh herbs, fruit, meat and vegtables are. It really puts food on another level.

For the beginners out there in regard to herbs, I recommend Parsley. Parsley is really the most important one of them all. It brings out the flavour of any dish. It is very versatile, you get away with a sprigling of it almost anywhere.

Sea salt and black pepper

To make sure your food is tasty, you have to make sure your ingrediants logically are tasty too. I buy sea salt, there are cheaper varietys but sea salt tastes the best. Well, ultimately the decision is yours but you should try different salts and make a decision. I encourage you to make little consumer tests on ingrediants as much as possible. Freshly ground black pepper is again a high recommendation from me.

Stock and sauce

Keep a little vegtable stock availible. A little bit of this concentrated goodness makes a good soup, a good sauce and ultimately a good meal.
